## 3. Which variables should not be over-interpreted?

The following variables should be interpreted carefully:

- **Average Discount Percentage** because discounts alone may not always increase sales.
- **Competitor Distance** because external market conditions may influence this relationship.

These variables may be affected by factors not included in the dataset.

---

## 5. What risks or limitations should leadership keep in mind?

Leadership should consider the following limitations:

- The model does not capture all external factors such as economic conditions, weather, or competitor strategies.
- The analysis is based on historical data and future patterns may differ.
- Seasonal effects may not be fully represented in the model.

---

## 6. Why does regression show association but not automatically prove causation?

Regression identifies relationships between variables but does not prove that one variable directly causes another.

For example, higher marketing spend may be associated with higher sales, but other factors such as store location, customer preferences, or seasonality may also influence sales.

Therefore, regression results should support business decisions but should not be considered as proof of cause-and-effect relationships.
