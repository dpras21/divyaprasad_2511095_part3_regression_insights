# Residual Analysis

## 1. Predicted Sales Calculation

Predicted sales were calculated using the final multiple regression model. The predicted values were generated for all 320 observations using the regression equation.

## 2. Residual Calculation

Residuals were calculated using the formula:

Residual = Actual Sales - Predicted Sales

Positive residuals indicate that actual sales were higher than predicted by the model, while negative residuals indicate that actual sales were lower than predicted.

## 3. Records with Largest Positive Residuals

Examples of observations with high positive residuals include:

| Observation | Residual |
|------------|-----------|
| Record 5 | 95,431.68 |
| Record 7 | 48,582.78 |
| Record 8 | 16,033.55 |
| Record 14 | 48,707.73 |
| Record 17 | 24,412.13 |

These stores performed better than expected.

## 4. Records with Largest Negative Residuals

Examples of observations with high negative residuals include:

| Observation | Residual |
|------------|-----------|
| Record 1 | -82,828.43 |
| Record 2 | -116,742.85 |
| Record 3 | -124,419.25 |
| Record 4 | -46,792.16 |
| Record 10 | -29,715.39 |

These stores performed below the model's expectations.

## 5. Business Interpretation

Large positive residuals suggest that some stores generated higher sales than predicted. This could be due to factors not included in the model, such as local promotions, festive demand, store location advantages, or exceptional customer service.

Large negative residuals suggest that some stores underperformed. Possible reasons include operational issues, local competition, poor customer experience, or external market factors not captured in the dataset.

## 6. Under-Prediction and Over-Prediction

The model both under-predicts and over-predicts sales for certain stores. Positive residuals indicate under-prediction, whereas negative residuals indicate over-prediction.

Overall, the residual values are reasonably distributed around zero, suggesting that the model performs well, although some unexplained variation still exists due to factors not included in the analysis.
